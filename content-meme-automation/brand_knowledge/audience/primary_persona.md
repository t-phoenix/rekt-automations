# Primary Persona: The "Rekt" Degen & Web3 Survivor

## Demographics & Core Identities
The Rekt CEO audience is broad but united by shared experiences in the trenches of Web3. The primary segments include:
*   **Traders & Degens:** Constantly chasing the next 100x gem, often holding bags down 90%.
*   **Corporate Employees / 9-5ers:** Multitasking between Excel sheets and ETH gas fees, seeking an escape from the rat race.
*   **Engineers, Founders & Builders:** Shipping code or products but still failing to time the market or getting rugged by bad tokenomics.
*   **NFT Collectors & Artists:** Tired of 1/1 art that doesn't sell and JPEGs that lose 99% of their floor price.
*   **Meme Lords & Internet Natives:** Creating viral content for free on Web2 and looking to monetize their cultural capital.
*   **Other Sub-groups:** Students, remote workers, ex-bankers, gamers, and anonymous lurkers who hide their faces but share their rekt trades.

## Psychographics & Motivations
*   **The 70/20/10 Rule:** Retail investors in this space are driven by three core desires: Making Money (70%), Having Fun (20%), and Belonging to a Tribe (10%).
*   **PvE Over PvP:** They are exhausted by "PvP" (Player vs Player) hypergambling and rotating through scam coins. They want a "PvE" (Player vs Environment) "cult"—a community they can buy and HODL, where diamond hands are rewarded.
*   **Disillusionment with "Tech":** They recognize that 99.99% of altcoins don't produce revenue to justify their valuations. They no longer care about promises of "faster blockchains" or "zk snark cryptography"; they understand that the *token* is the real product and the *community* is the utility.
*   **Humor as a Coping Mechanism:** They deal with the trauma of financial loss by memeing it. They are transitioning from hiding their losses to owning them as a badge of honor ("From Rekt to Ruler").
*   **Craving Ownership:** They want to stop being the product on traditional social media and start truly owning their digital identity and creations (IP).
